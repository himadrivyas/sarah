Case study

1080 Colour Renderings

The task

3M Canada needed to increase sales of their Wrap Film Series. They were originally displaying these products out of package with the transparent roll on a white background. The business was getting feedback from current users that they would be more inclined to purchase the product if they could see it applied to a vehicle. I then pitched to the business about having a 3D rendering created. With this idea in place I would be able to upload the existing colour swatch into the program and design a wrap material that could then be laid onto an automotive model. The business agreed that this was the perfect solution and would not only complete the task of creating 113 new images but would also create the opportunity to create new renderings when new colours continued to be released.

Existing layout

New layout

Biggest challenge

The biggest challenge with this project was having the digital version match the colour swatch that was already photographed. There was a lot of photo editing between Photoshop and the rendering software KeyShot to precisely nail the end colour. And once the colour was finally on point we had to play with the lighting and see if it was registering the right colour for the full 360 model as it spun. After a couple of long weeks I was able to finally send the working files to the digitization team to upload to the 3M Canada website.

The result

With application photos being uploaded we saw our bounce rate decrease and our clickthrough rate increase within the next month! With these milestones met, we started to create these renderings for more than just the 1080 Wrap Film Series portfolio. We also created renderings for our Vinyl Graphics and Reflective Films portfolios.
