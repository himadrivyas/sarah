Case study

Tensor™ Brand

The task

Tensor Brand wanted to revamp their web design with a new UX/UI. They wanted a design inspired by their US counterpart's site, so I introduced several improvements to streamline and enhance the user experience.

United States

Canada

Biggest challenge

I wanted to introduce a product selector to help customers find the best product that fit their needs. To identify the most effective way to present the products, I collaborated with another designer and a user researcher and mapped out the customer journey. We discovered that most users “self-diagnose” their injuries and search for suitable products that address those injuries. Additionally, competitor analysis revealed that none of our competitors approached the user experience from this perspective, so we decided to seize that opportunity.

The solution

I ultimately designed an interactive product selector that was engaging and easy to use, organized primarily by injury. I also included quick search functionality for users who know exactly which products they are looking for.

Designing a natural, intuitive user flow was key to ensuring the success of the product selector.

What would I change

I would change the navigation at the top to show the current selection made by the user. I took the initiative to have some close family members and friends (varying in ages) and found that almost everyone forgot the categories they selected and started to panic once they got further into the product selector. If the users were able to see each selection made, than I believe they would be more confident with the end result.

Current live product selector

My updates

With the selection now included in the top navigation, the user can continue the path to purchase confidentially.
